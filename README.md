SQL data mining and reporting
To extract business-critical information from large relational databases to support strategic reporting.
Executed complex SQL queries (joins, subqueries, and aggregations) for data mining and designed optimized database schemas for specific business inquiries.
Successfully retrieved and structured high-volume data, reducing the time required for generating monthly performance reports.

# Project Overview
In late 2025, I spearheaded a data transformation initiative to bridge the gap between massive, fragmented relational datasets and actionable business strategy. By leveraging the Airline and Northwind databases, I developed a robust framework for extracting high-value insights, focusing on operational efficiency and revenue optimization.
The Challenge
 - The organization faced a common "big data" hurdle: critical information was siloed across complex tables, making monthly performance reporting a slow, manual, and error-prone process. The goal was to move beyond basic data retrieval and create a system capable of answering complex business questions—such as identifying low-margin shipping routes and predicting aircraft maintenance windows—with speed and precision.
- Technical Execution & Strategy
- Deep-Dive Mining: I utilized advanced SQL techniques, including multi-level subqueries and complex window functions, to perform deep-tissue data mining. For the Airline dataset, this meant aggregating millions of flight records to isolate factors affecting "on-time" performance. For Northwind, it involved analyzing supplier lead times against customer demand.
- Schema Optimization: Recognizing that standard schemas aren't always built for speed, I designed optimized database views and custom schemas. By implementing strategic indexing and refining table relationships (Normalization/3NF), I significantly reduced query execution time for high-volume transactions.
- Aggregated Reporting: I built logic-heavy scripts that automated the heavy lifting of data aggregation. This allowed for the seamless merging of disparate data points—like linking passenger loyalty tiers with flight profitability metrics—into a unified reporting format.
The Impact
- The results were immediate and measurable:
Reporting Velocity: We achieved a significant reduction in the time required to generate monthly performance reports, shifting the workload from manual data preparation to high-level analysis.
Strategic Clarity: The "Business-Critical" insights retrieved led to a more granular understanding of operational bottlenecks, allowing leadership to make data-backed adjustments to supply chain logistics and flight scheduling.
- Data Integrity: The optimized schema design minimized data redundancy and ensured that strategic reports were built on a consistent, "single source of truth."

- Conclusion
This project demonstrated my ability to navigate complex relational environments and translate "data noise" into a structured asset. By optimizing the underlying architecture and the queries that interact with it, I turned a standard database into a strategic engine for the business.
